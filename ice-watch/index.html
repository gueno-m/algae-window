Ice Watch, 2014 - 2015, 2018 - 2019

In the Arctic, the area covered by the ice decreases by approximately 13% per decade for 40 years.

The increase in temperature air is responsible for melting ice. Especially at the poles, the air heats up faster than elsewhere, partly because of the high concentration of greenhouse gases.

The melting ice is the main cause of rising waters. Since 1901, the sea level has risen by about 20 cm and the pace picks up.
